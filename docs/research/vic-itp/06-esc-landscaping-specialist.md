## Template 23: Erosion & Sediment Control (ESC)

```
Template Name: Erosion & Sediment Control
Activity Type: erosion_control
Specification Reference: VicRoads Section 176 (Environmental Management - Minor) / Section 177 (Environmental Management - Major)
Edition/Revision Date: Section 176 v6 (May 2015), Section 177 v5 (May 2016) [VERIFY current versions]
```

### Referenced Standards
- VicRoads Section 176 -- Environmental Management (Minor)
- VicRoads Section 177 -- Environmental Management (Major)
- EPA Victoria Publication 275 -- Construction Techniques for Sediment Pollution Control
- IECA Australasia -- Best Practice Erosion and Sediment Control (BPESC)
- EPA Victoria Environmental Reference Standard (ERS)

### Notes on Specification
VicRoads does not have a standalone ESC specification section. Erosion and sediment control requirements are embedded within:
- Section 176 (Environmental Management -- Minor) for smaller works
- Section 177 (Environmental Management -- Major) for larger works
These are supplemented by EPA Victoria Publication 275 and IECA Australasia best practice guidelines. Project-specific ESC plans are typically required under the contract Environmental Management Plan (EMP).

### Notes on Specification
VicRoads Section 210 covers geotextiles used as separation and filtration layers in earthworks. Geotextiles for subsurface drainage applications are additionally covered by Section 702. For geogrid and geomembrane applications, project-specific specifications typically supplement Section 210. VicRoads classifies geotextiles by robustness class (Class A through D), with Class A for moderately robust and Class D for extremely robust applications.

### Notes on Specification
**Section 409 (Warm Mix Asphalt) has been retired.** Warm mix asphalt requirements are now covered within Section 407 (Hot Mix Asphalt).
